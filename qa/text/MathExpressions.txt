Amazon Pay — Financial and Offer Math (Linear expressions)

Definitions:
PurchaseAmount = total amount the customer pays before offers and taxes.
Tax = PurchaseAmount * TaxRate
OfferCashback = PurchaseAmount * CashbackRate
NetCharge = PurchaseAmount - OfferDiscount - OfferCashback + Tax

Examples:
1) Simple cashback calculation:
   PurchaseAmount = 1000
   CashbackRate = 0.05
   OfferCashback = 1000 * 0.05 = 50

2) Applying promo discount and cashback:
   PurchaseAmount = 2500
   PromoDiscount = 200
   CashbackRate = 0.03
   OfferCashback = (PurchaseAmount - PromoDiscount) * 0.03
                = (2500 - 200) * 0.03
                = 2300 * 0.03 = 69
   TaxRate = 0.18
   Tax = (PurchaseAmount - PromoDiscount) * TaxRate = 2300 * 0.18 = 414
   NetCharge = PurchaseAmount - PromoDiscount - OfferCashback + Tax
             = 2500 - 200 - 69 + 414 = 2645

3) Merchant payout example (simplified):
   GrossSale = sum of captured amounts
   AmazonFee = GrossSale * FeeRate
   NetPayout = GrossSale - AmazonFee - Refunds - Chargebacks

4) Recurring billing example:
   MonthlyCharge = BaseAmount + (Usage * UnitRate)
   AnnualEstimate = MonthlyCharge * 12

Notes:
- Use linear expressions like above to model cashback, taxes, discounts, and merchant settlements.
- Round monetary results to the correct currency precision (e.g., 2 decimal places).
