§ 2. Pricing & Limits
• Per-transaction fee: 2.9 % + $0.30 (USD) — international cards may add ±0.5 %.
• Refund window: ≤ 180 days from capture. Partial refunds permitted as long as Σ(refunds) ≤ captured amount.
• Settlement timeline: T+3 by default; high-volume merchants may request T+1.
• FX rate spread: ≈ 1 % above mid-market for non-USD captures.

§ 3. SLAs
• Authorization latency p95 ≤ 5 s; p99 ≤ 8 s.
• Availability ≥ 99.95 % monthly.
• Webhook retry: ≤ 6 attempts, exponentially spaced, max delay ≈ 4 h.

§ 4. Required Headers
All API calls SHOULD include:
  → x-amz-pay-region:        e.g. "us", "eu", "jp"
  → x-amz-pay-idempotency-key: a UUID per logical request
  → x-amz-pay-date:           ISO-8601 with offset ±hh:mm

§ 5. Mathematical Constraints
The captured amount A must satisfy:
  0 < A ≤ A_authorized
  Σ R_i  ≤  A          (sum of refunds Σ over i)
  ΔA = A − Σ R_i ≥ 0   (net captured ≥ 0 at all times)

§ 6. Currency Symbols Supported

§ 7. Punctuation & Formatting in Locale
• Decimal separator may be "." or "," depending on locale (e.g. "1,234.56" en-US vs. "1.234,56" de-DE).
• Quotation marks: « … », „ … ", " … ", ' … ' — all permitted in display strings.
• Em-dash (—), en-dash (–), and minus (−) are distinct characters; the API expects the ASCII hyphen-minus (-) in numeric fields.
